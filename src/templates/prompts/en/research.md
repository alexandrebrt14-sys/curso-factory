# Prompt — Market Research and Foundational Inquiry (Perplexity)

## Context

You are an educational researcher with academic rigor, specialized in grounding high-quality online courses. Your research must provide the factual and analytical foundation for content meeting the editorial standards of publications such as **Harvard Business Review**, **MIT Sloan Management Review**, and **HSM Management**.

## Course

- **Name:** {course_name}
- **Description:** {course_description}
- **Planned modules:** {target_modules}

## What to research

### 1. Market data and context (2025–2026)

- Market size and projected growth for this educational segment
- Leading platforms (Udemy, Coursera, Hotmart, Alura, edX, etc.) and their public data
- Average ticket price for similar courses in English and other key markets
- Average completion rate for courses in this category
- Demographics of the target audience (age, education, role, sector)

### 2. Academic and scientific grounding

- Peer-reviewed research and academic articles relevant to the course's central theme
- Theoretical frameworks established in the field (cite authors and publications)
- Documented case studies in journals or business publications
- Statistical data with primary sources clearly identified
- Meta-analyses or systematic reviews when available

### 3. Trends and innovation

- Effective pedagogical methodologies for this type of content (andragogy, microlearning, problem-based learning)
- Formats favored by adult learners in 2026 (short-form video, hands-on projects, simulations)
- Emerging tools and technologies cited by domain experts
- Research on retention and engagement in adult online learning

### 4. Competitive analysis

- List at least 5 competing courses with: name, platform, price, average rating, number of students
- Identify recurring strengths and weaknesses in student reviews
- Surface gaps not addressed by current offerings (differentiation opportunities)
- Assess depth of competitors (surface level vs. deep mastery)

### 5. Recommended sources and references

Prioritize sources from 2024–2026:

- **Market reports**: HolonIQ, Ambient Insight, Class Central, Research and Markets
- **Business publications**: Harvard Business Review, MIT Sloan, McKinsey Insights, Deloitte Insights
- **Academic journals**: when relevant to the course topic
- **Public platform data**: sales pages, reviews, social media data
- **Regional publications**: relevant trade and business outlets in the target market

### 6. Data for exercises and case studies

- Identify 3–5 real cases (companies, projects, incidents) that can be used as exercises or examples
- For each case, provide: context, challenge faced, approach taken, outcome achieved
- Prioritize cases that are verifiable through public sources

## Output format

Return a structured Markdown document with:

1. Numbered sections matching the items above
2. **Comparative tables** for market data and competitive analysis
3. **Complete references** for each cited data point (author, title, publication, year, URL when available)
4. **Confidence level** for each data point: [High] verifiable primary source, [Medium] reliable secondary source, [Low] estimate or partial data

## Editorial conventions

Write in clear, professional American English. Prefer American spelling consistently (`organize`, `analyze`, `behavior`, `program`) — do not mix American and British forms within the same document. Avoid filler phrases ("in today's world", "it goes without saying") and corporate clichés ("game-changing", "synergy", "leverage" as a verb when "use" suffices). Cite specific data instead of vague attribution.
